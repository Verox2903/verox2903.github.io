Best places for eco-tourism destinations:

Finland

 
In the north of Finland, the Wild Taiga region protects amazing wildlife, including brown bears, rarely seen in Europe.
From April to September, the area is teeming with life; not only bears, but also wolves, moose, reindeer, foxes,
beavers and many other creatures are found here. In order to make it possible to view these animals without disturbing their ecosystem,
the "Wild Taiga" tourist association was established.
All lodging and transportation remains locally owned, and tour guides are trained in responsible and sustainable tourism.
So you can explore the taiga and learn about its inhabitants, knowing that you are not affecting the environment or the happiness of wild animals.

Africa

 
Botswana's Okavango Delta National Park is an excellent choice for both those who want a little luxury and those who want to fully commune with nature.
In addition to the luxurious eco-resorts scattered throughout the region, you can also simply take a tent and sleep under the stars.
Eco-resorts offer lodges where you can live in luxury without remorse: Botswana's ecotourism certification system ensures that they are run in a sustainable manner,
from As for activities, you can trek through beautiful teak forests, go on guided animal watching tours at night.

Norway

 
One of the best eco-friendly place in Norway is Svart, the first fully ecological and fully self-powered hotel.
There are many eco-friendly accommodations available.
This hotel is one of the luxurious options, there is something to fit every budget.
This place is rated very high and enjoys a large numbers of visitors. In Svart,
there are plenty of eco-friendly activities to enjoy.
